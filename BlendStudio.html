This photograph is made up of 11 sections, each section consists of a stack of 119 raw photographs each at 100μm* apart this is a total of 1,309 photographs. For the final image I had to remove hundreds of dust particles on the insect and removed the background replacing it with a solid colour and a drop shadow all using Adobe Photoshop. 

*μm is the measurment the stacking system uses. A human hair is 70 μm
Here is a flower from the Horse chestnut tree. Inflorescence is the word to describe a cluster of flowers arranged on a single stem. I counted the flowers and there are 7 to a stalk and 35 of these on a stem. Thats 240 flowers in one cluster. Each flower turns into a conker. Using Adobe Photoshop I removed dust and pollen particles and removed the background, replacing it with a solid colour.
This image is an artists shoot commisioned by https://www.procreateproject.com/. The final image is a series of 63 individual photo's that had been colour matched in Adobe Photoshop and stiched together in Autopano giga. The final image will be printed by Concord Graphics and mounted on 8'x4' aluminium composite panel displayed in public for Deptford X, 2021.
